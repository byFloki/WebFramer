Why Choose a Static Website?

Static sites offer powerful advantages, especially for cost-conscious clients.

Blazing Fast Speed

No database lookups or complex processing. Files are served instantly, improving user experience and SEO.

Enhanced Security

No server-side code or databases means a dramatically smaller attack surface. Much harder to hack.

Lower Costs

Free hosting options (like GitHub Pages) and simpler development mean significant cost savings.

Simplicity & Reliability

Fewer moving parts mean less can go wrong. Highly reliable and easy to maintain.

Affordable & Transparent Pricing

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a "static" website?

A static website consists of pre-built HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files that are delivered directly to the user's browser exactly as stored. Unlike dynamic sites (like WordPress), they don't need a database or server-side processing to generate content on the fly. This makes them inherently faster, more secure, and cheaper to host.

How long does it take to build a website?

Turnaround time depends on the project complexity and how quickly you provide content/feedback. A typical small site (1-3 pages) often takes 1-5 days from design approval to launch, assuming clear communication.

Can I update the website myself later?

Updating content on a static site usually requires editing the HTML code directly. For simple text changes, we can show you how if you're comfortable. For significant updates or if you prefer not to touch code, we offer affordable maintenance packages or can perform updates on an hourly basis.

Alternatively, for sites requiring frequent updates by non-technical users, we can integrate a 'headless CMS' (like Contentful, Sanity, or Decap CMS) for an additional fee, allowing you to edit content through a user-friendly interface.

What do you need from me to start?

To start, we need a clear idea of your goals, target audience, the content (text, images, logo) for each page, any design preferences or examples you like, and your domain name ideas (if you don't have one yet).
